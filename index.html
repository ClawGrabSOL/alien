U.S. Government Acquires "alien.gov" Domain as White House Confirms Discovery of Extraterrestrial Species

In an unprecedented move, federal authorities registered the domain alien.gov Tuesday morning — hours after the White House confirmed the existence of a previously unknown extraterrestrial species recovered from a 2019 deep-sea expedition near the Mariana Trench.
By Margaret L. Harmon, Senior Science Correspondent  |  David K. Wren, Washington Bureau  |  Reuters
March 18, 2026  ·  11:47 AM EDT  ·  Updated 1:22 PM EDT
A composite rendering of the extraterrestrial organism, designated Velon, based on classified imaging data released under Emergency Disclosure Protocol 7. — U.S. Department of Advanced Research / Reuters

WASHINGTON, D.C. — The United States government formally registered the domain alien.gov at 6:14 AM EDT Tuesday, a move that preceded a landmark White House press briefing in which Press Secretary Dana Kimbell confirmed what millions have long suspected: humanity is not alone.

The announcement came on the heels of a controlled declassification of documents relating to Operation DEEP WITNESS, a classified DARPA-funded expedition that recovered biological specimens from a depth of 36,000 feet in the Mariana Trench in September 2019. Those specimens, kept under strict containment at a facility in Nevada, have now been confirmed by a panel of 34 independent scientists as representing a previously unknown form of sentient extraterrestrial life.

"What we have recovered, studied, and now confirmed is not of this Earth. We believe this species has existed alongside us — largely undetected — for longer than our civilization itself."
— Dr. Alan Forsythe, Director, DARPA Biological Systems Division

The species, formally designated Velon by the scientific community, stands approximately 1.8 meters tall, is bioluminescent under ultraviolet light, and exhibits a neurological structure estimated to be 400% more complex than that of a human adult. Colloquially, researchers who worked with the specimens referred to the beings as "Velons."

What Is Known About Velon

According to declassified briefing documents obtained by Reuters, Velon is a carbon-based lifeform with bilateral symmetry and a four-chambered cardiovascular system. Unlike humans, the species possesses no visible external auditory organs, instead relying on a dense network of electroreceptors embedded beneath a semi-translucent dermal layer to perceive both sound and electromagnetic fields simultaneously.

The specimen recovered in 2019 — referred to internally as Subject AX-7741 — was non-hostile and displayed what researchers described as deliberate, communicative behavior within 72 hours of containment. A team of linguists, mathematicians, and neurologists spent the following three years attempting to establish a shared communication framework.

Declassified Excerpt — Operation Deep Witness, Internal Report #DW-2021-114 Subject AX-7741 demonstrated unprompted mathematical output on Day 4 of containment, inscribing prime number sequences on observation surfaces using a pigment secreted from its palmar glands. By Day 11, the subject had replicated the periodic table in its entirety from memory after a single 40-minute exposure to printed materials.

Cognitive assessment: OFF-SCALE. Recommend immediate escalation to Tier-1 classified status.

— Dr. R. Chambers, Lead Cognitive Researcher, Clearance Level: UMBRA

Senior government officials declined to confirm whether AX-7741 remains alive, citing ongoing national security considerations. However, three sources with direct knowledge of the program told Reuters that the specimen is "stable, cooperative, and has been an active participant in communications efforts."

Why alien.gov?

The decision to register and activate alien.gov was made, according to a senior White House official, to establish a single, authoritative government source for public information about the disclosure — and to counter what officials described as an anticipated flood of misinformation across social media platforms.

"The domain will serve as the official home of the Extraterrestrial Disclosure Task Force," said Press Secretary Kimbell during Tuesday's briefing. "All verified information, scientific updates, and public guidance will be published there. We are asking the public to treat any other source with extreme caution."

The domain was registered through the General Services Administration at 6:14 AM, and a temporary landing page was activated at 9:00 AM. A full information portal is expected to go live within 72 hours, officials said.

International Reaction

The announcement triggered immediate responses from world leaders. The United Nations Secretary-General called an emergency session of the Security Council for Wednesday morning. The European Space Agency issued a statement saying it had been "informally briefed" by U.S. counterparts 48 hours prior. China's foreign ministry, in a terse statement, demanded "full multilateral transparency" and suggested the U.S. had violated several provisions of the 1967 Outer Space Treaty.

Scientists not affiliated with the program described the announcement as "the most significant event in the history of our species."

"Every paradigm we have — religious, philosophical, scientific — shifts today," said Dr. Priya Nair, professor of astrobiology at MIT. "There is no version of this that leaves anything unchanged."

What Happens Next

The White House confirmed that President Morrison will address the nation in a primetime broadcast at 9:00 PM EDT Tuesday. Congress has been briefed in a closed session. The Department of Defense is expected to release a separate statement regarding any implications for national security.

The alien.gov portal, when fully operational, will include a live Q&A section, a scientific data repository, and a portal for credentialed researchers to apply for access to non-classified study materials.

For now, the world waits.
